Warrior Athlete Strength Badge  

USMC Sgt. Enrique Treviño’s bold goal in 2012 to raise funds for the Wounded Warrior Project– a million push-ups in 1 year.

WA interviewed Sgt. Treviño last month and this is what he had to say.

WA—Back in 2012, you made a bold move. Your pledge meant 2,732 push-ups per day and nearly 114 every hour. How did you manage this?

Sgt. T--I had personal doubts the first day. I think I was half way through, and my body was spent, and I thought, ‘I've got to wake up tomorrow and do this again with 364 days left in the year.’

WA—But exactly how did you do this without overtraining? I mean, as a Warrior Athlete, you must have been aware of the injuries you could have sustained. Right?

 Sgt. T—Sure. I was very careful to have a balanced, progressive loading exercise program, like Lt. General Hertling talked about in the video. In the end, I designed 40-minute workout blocks at lunch to complete between 750 and 850 push-ups safely. Through the rest of the day, I hit the deck every 15 or 20 minutes while my Gunnery Sgt. supervised. Gunny made me do at least 50 before standing up. I also did push-ups every commercial break at home while watching TV.

WA—Thanks, Sgt. Treviño. You are a great example to aspire to. –end.
